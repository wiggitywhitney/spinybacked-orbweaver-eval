# Platypus Facts

The platypus is one of only five species of monotremes — mammals that lay eggs. Native to eastern Australia, it has a duck-like bill, beaver-like tail, and venomous spurs on its hind legs. The male's venom is powerful enough to cause severe pain in humans.

Platypuses hunt underwater using electroreception — their bills detect the tiny electric fields generated by the muscle contractions of prey. They close their eyes, ears, and nostrils while diving and navigate entirely by electrical signals.

Despite looking like an evolutionary prank, the platypus genome contains genes from mammals, birds, and reptiles, making it a living bridge between major vertebrate lineages.
