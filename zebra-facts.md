# Zebra Facts

No two zebras have the same stripe pattern — each is as unique as a human fingerprint. Researchers use stripe pattern recognition software to identify individual zebras in the wild without tagging them.

Zebra stripes may function as a natural insect repellent. Studies show that horseflies are less likely to land on striped surfaces than solid-colored ones. The leading theory is that the stripes disrupt the polarization pattern of light that flies use to identify landing surfaces.

A group of zebras is called a "dazzle." When a dazzle runs together, their overlapping stripe patterns create a visual confusion effect that makes it harder for predators to single out one individual — a form of motion camouflage.
